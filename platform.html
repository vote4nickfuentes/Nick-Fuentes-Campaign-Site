A Senate Agenda Rooted in Experience

“Public office is a public trust.”

Philosophy must translate into policy. Between 2002 and 2004, I worked to demonstrate that reform can be achieved without extremism and that constitutional governance can coexist with decisive action.

My Senate platform builds directly upon the legislation already enacted during my tenure as House Minority Leader — expanding proven reforms to strengthen national stability, accountability, and institutional trust.

America First Means Economic Security

Economic security is not theory — it is policy enacted into law. As House Minority Leader, I introduced and passed the New Deal for American Stability Act of 2003, a targeted reform focused on job creation, payroll tax relief, domestic investment incentives, housing supply expansion, and infrastructure modernization.

That legislation proved that economic intervention can be temporary, disciplined, and fiscally responsible — without creating permanent entitlement expansion.

  • Payroll tax relief for job creation
  • State-led infrastructure block grants with private capital participation
  • Housing supply incentives tied to regulatory reform
  • Strict five-year sunset and $55B spending cap for fiscal discipline

National Recovery Through American Industry

The New Deal for American Stability Act strengthened domestic production and removed regulatory bottlenecks that slowed growth. It prioritized transportation, energy, logistics, and advanced manufacturing — ensuring that recovery meant structural resilience, not short-term stimulus.

In the Senate, I will expand domestic investment incentives while preserving state control, regulatory modernization, and economic competitiveness.

  • Infrastructure investment using American labor and materials
  • Streamlined federal permitting to reduce economic delay
  • Investment tax credits for domestic expansion and modernization
  • Regulatory review safeguards to ensure efficiency without repeal by default

Responsible Government, Not Weak Government

Responsible government means acting decisively while preserving constitutional limits. The New Deal for American Stability Act included strict fiscal safeguards — no permanent entitlements, no deficit funding, and mandatory sunset provisions.

Reform without restraint is instability. Inaction without responsibility is failure. My legislative record demonstrates that government can act — and then step back.

  • Spending caps and automatic sunset provisions
  • No creation of permanent mandatory spending programs
  • Funding sourced through surplus and growth, not structural deficit expansion

Integrity, Law, and Democratic Trust

Public trust is strengthened when laws are clear, fair, and constitutionally grounded. The Firearms Responsibility, Rights, and Public Safety Act of 2003 protected Second Amendment rights while prioritizing enforcement against violent offenders.

That legislation clarified federal firearms law, protected due process in mental health adjudications, and preserved state authority — proving that liberty and order are not opposites.

  • Interstate concealed carry recognition with state authority preserved
  • Affirmative reliance defense for citizens following DOJ guidance
  • Focused federal-state task force grants targeting violent offenders
  • Due process protections and judicial review safeguards

Public Safety and National Stability

Public safety policy must target criminal misuse, not lawful ownership. The Firearms Responsibility Act prioritized prosecution of violent offenders and firearms trafficking — without expanding federal surveillance or creating new blanket prohibitions.

Stability comes from enforcement consistency and constitutional clarity — not regulatory confusion.

  • Priority prosecution of felons unlawfully possessing firearms
  • Task force support for enforcement of existing law
  • Voluntary firearm safety incentives without mandates
  • Explicit prohibition on creation of a federal registry

Education Accountability and Opportunity

The No Child Left Behind Act of 2003, enacted during my tenure as House Minority Leader, established measurable statewide standards, annual assessments, and transparent reporting to parents.

Education reform requires accountability paired with local flexibility. The law strengthened standards while preserving state-level implementation authority.

  • Clear statewide academic standards in reading, mathematics, and science
  • Annual assessment transparency with publicly reported results
  • Adequate Yearly Progress benchmarks and corrective action plans
  • Teacher qualification standards and parental reporting requirements

America First in the World

America’s foreign policy must serve the security and prosperity of our people. A strong national defense is essential, but engagement abroad must remain grounded in national interest and constitutional authority.

Domestic strength — economic, institutional, and civic — is the foundation of credible global leadership.

  • A strong, prepared national defense
  • Diplomacy grounded in national interest and credibility
  • Avoidance of unnecessary foreign entanglements
