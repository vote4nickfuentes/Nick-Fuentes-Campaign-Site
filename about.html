Service to the Public Trust

“Public office is a public trust.”

This campaign is rooted in a lifelong commitment to public service and the belief that democratic government must be competent, principled, and worthy of the confidence placed in it by the American people.

A Record of Public Service

My career in public life has been shaped by service across every branch of government, guided by a simple belief: public authority exists to serve the American people, not itself.

Each office I have held carried distinct responsibilities, but all shared a common purpose — stewardship of the public trust and a responsibility to put America first through effective, principled governance.

House Minority Leader (2002–2004)

Serving as House Minority Leader required legislative discipline, coalition-building, and strategic restraint. Despite operating without majority control, three major legislative proposals were enacted into law during my tenure.

That experience affirmed that effective leadership is not measured by volume, but by results.

State Attorney General

As State Attorney General, I served as the chief legal officer of the state, charged with enforcing the law, defending the public interest, and ensuring that justice was administered fairly and without favor.

The office demanded independence and sound judgment, particularly when political pressure conflicted with the rule of law. Upholding the law — even when difficult — was not optional; it was the duty of the office.

Lieutenant Governor

In my service as Lieutenant Governor, I worked closely with executive leadership and legislators to guide policy, oversee government operations, and represent the state during moments requiring steady and responsible leadership.

That experience reinforced the importance of cooperation, accountability, and administrative competence in democratic government.

Federal Judge

My service on the federal bench required a deliberate separation from politics and a disciplined commitment to the fair and impartial interpretation of the law.

Each case was approached with respect for constitutional limits, judicial independence, and the obligation to decide matters based on law rather than passion or pressure.

Chief Justice of the Court of Appeals

Serving as Chief Justice of the Court of Appeals carried responsibility not only for individual decisions, but for the leadership and integrity of the judiciary itself.

In that role, I worked to ensure that the courts functioned efficiently, consistently, and with the confidence of the public they serve.

Why I Am Running

I am running because moments of national uncertainty demand experience, judgment, and steady leadership. Having served in executive, legal, and judicial capacities, I understand how government functions — and where it can fail the people it exists to serve.

This campaign is not about ideology or ambition. It is about responsibility: governing with seriousness, competence, and a clear commitment to putting America first through strong, trusted institutions.
